
# ios-audio-gatekeeping-study

Background: A problem arises when an increasing number of mechanisms disrupt the free market for niche products (in this case, the iOS audio market) in a variety of ways. This ranges from (more or less) incomplete listings to active market manipulation by self-proclaimed experts and questionable, self-serving communities – but also by individual players who profit from the mechanisms.

#### General information regarding the validity of data collection: All underlying datasets are considered frozen as of the last update date of this repository.

### 🚨 Case Study (extraordinary): Real-Time Empirical Proof of Forum Censorship (July 2026)

To demonstrate that the "Shadow Governance" described in this study is actively suppressing independent research, we document a live gatekeeping event involving the *://loopypro.com* ecosystem:

#### The Event:
A direct link to this repository was posted on the forum. Within hours, platform moderators actively targeted and **deleted the official URL** to prevent organic discovery by the general user base.

#### The Dashboard Anomalies (Metrical Analysis):
- **Referring Site Metrics:** ://loopypro.com
- **Unique Visitors:** 3
- **Total Views:** 51

#### Structural Interpretation:
This extreme statistical distortion (an average of 17 page-views per unique visitor) provides definitive proof of targeted administrative surveillance. The general public was blocked from accessing the link, while a closed inner circle of exactly three moderators repeatedly scanned, monitored, and scrutinized this repository to evaluate its threat level to their centralized curation monopoly before executing the deletion.

**Conclusion:** The gatekeepers do not just filter software products; they actively censor the empirical research that exposes their economic and social manipulation.

# Transparency

Regarding data collection transparency: All results are generated using Apple's official public iTunes query API. This API is subject to strict limitations. Frequently used search terms are entered, and the results are stored and updated in a global database. Lists and feeds are then generated by querying this global database, applying global filters as comprehensively as possible to deliver the most relevant results for mobile music production.

### Note: It's noticeable that certain developers and products are extremely difficult to find here, as Apple apparently uses internal filters that are opaque and virtually impossible for outsiders to access. Therefore, it cannot be guaranteed that the currently very extensive data collection is complete.

Please also note, that apps meight have applied local availability restrictions, set by the developers.
