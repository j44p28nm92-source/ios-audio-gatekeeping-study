[Study 2](./study%202.md)

Abstract: This paper explores the dark side of niche digital platforms, where software support forums evolve into tools of shadow market governance. Utilizing a qualitative discourse analysis of a specialized online community, the study investigates a toxic forum bubble whose leaders act as a self-styled regulatory police force. Lacking verified technical or musical expertise, these actors rely on expertise-simulation and coercive group dynamics, heavily driven by the Dunning-Kruger effect and group polarization, to enforce arbitrary price and design standards. Through active gatekeeping, manipulative product framing, and the strategic recruitment or social boycutt of independent developers, they establish a parasitic ecosystem. This architecture is designed to redirect market visibility and revenue toward favored actors while systematically deconstructing and branding non-compliant creators. Ultimately, this case study uncovers how informal cartels in isolated digital environments weaponize anonymous crowd dynamics to execute market regulation, threatening the financial survival and creative autonomy of independent developers in a free creative market.Keywords: Shadow Market Governance, Inverse Gatekeeping, Expertise-Simulation, Dunning-Kruger Effect, Toxic Digital Bubbles, Niche Market Regulation.
